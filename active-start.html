ACTIVE START

Focus: Discovering Tennis

From the first shot to the highest levels, the Whole Player Development Pathway (WPDP) provides recommendations for various development stages and pathways. The Active Start stage introduces young players to the fundamentals of tennis through fun, engaging activities. This foundational phase focuses on developing basic motor skills, hand-eye coordination, and a love for the game.

How to Spot This Level

  • Players are new to tennis or have minimal experience
  • Focus on basic movement, balance, and coordination
  • Learning fundamental skills through games and play
  • Using modified equipment (smaller courts, lower nets, lighter balls)
  • Emphasis on fun, participation, and positive experiences
  • Players typically ages 5-8 years old
